FAMILY BIOGRAPHER

Privacy Policy

Last updated: July 13, 2026

Family Biographer records people telling their life stories. That is intimate material, and we treat it that way. This page explains, in plain language, what we collect and what we do with it.

What we collect

  • Account details — your name, email address, and a password (stored only in encrypted, hashed form).
  • Voice recordings — the storyteller's answers during interview sessions, recorded so they can be preserved and transcribed.
  • Transcripts and summaries — text created from those recordings, used to write the biography and choose the next question.
  • Basic details about the storyteller — a name and any background you choose to provide.

How we use it

  • To conduct the interviews, preserve the recordings, and produce the biography — that is the product.
  • To operate accounts, gifts, and customer support.

We do not sell your data. We do not use your recordings or stories for advertising.

Who else touches the data

We use a small number of service providers to run Family Biographer: cloud hosting for storage and delivery, a speech-to-text provider to transcribe recordings, and an AI provider to help choose interview questions and draft summaries. They process the data to provide the service to us and are not permitted to use it for their own purposes.

Where it lives and how long

Recordings, transcripts, and account data are stored on servers in the United States. We keep them for as long as your account is active, because preserving these stories is the point of the service. You can ask us to delete some or all of it at any time.

Your choices

  • The storyteller can skip any question, end any session, and is always told they are being recorded.
  • Email us to access, export, or permanently delete your recordings, transcripts, or account.

Children

Family Biographer is intended for adults and is not directed at children under 13.

Changes

If this policy changes in a way that matters, we will post the new version here and update the date at the top.
